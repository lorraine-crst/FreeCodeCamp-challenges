Margaret Hamilton: rocket woman

Stories of woman who made history at NASA

Here's a time line of Dra. Margaret life:

  • 1936- was born in Paoli, Indiana
  • 1954- Margaret graduated from Hancock High School
  • 1958- she earned a BA in mathematics with a minor in philosophy
  • 1958- While at Earlham, Hamilton met her first husband, James Cox Hamilton
  • 1959- they had a daughter, Lauren, born on November 10, 1959
  • 1959- Hamilton began working for Edward Norton Lorenz, in the meteorology department at MIT.
  • From 1961 to 1963 - Hamilton worked on the Semi-Automatic Ground Environment (SAGE) Project at the MIT Lincoln Lab
  • Apollo11- the Apollo Guidance Computer together with the on-board flight software averted an abort of the landing on the Moon. Three minutes before the lunar lander reached the Moon's surface, several computer alarms were triggered. The on-board flight software captured these alarms with the "never supposed to happen displays" interrupting the astronauts with priority alarm displays.
  • Em 1976 - Hamilton co-founded with Saydean Zeldin a company called Higher Order Software (HOS)[46] to further develop ideas about error prevention and fault tolerance emerging from their experience at MIT working on the Apollo program
  • 2021- On the day this site was made she is still alive, 85 years old
